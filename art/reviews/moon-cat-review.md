# Moon Cat Art Review

- Review date: 2026-07-13
- Reviewer: Codex, designated art verification lead
- Master canvas: 2048 × 2048 RGBA
- Layer pack: 8 named same-canvas layers
- Result: Approved for animation and runtime export

## Master feature check

1. The cat is an original compact floating companion with a calm, mysterious expression: pass.
2. Deep violet and silver-white dominate, with restrained pale-blue and moon-gold accents: pass.
3. Both pale-blue eyes are clear, consistent, and visually harmonize with the magical girl: pass.
4. The forehead uses an original lunar-phase arrangement rather than an existing character mark: pass.
5. The complete tail is visible and its tip forms a natural crescent silhouette: pass.
6. Stardust gradients remain restrained at the ears and paws: pass.
7. All four legs, both ears, the neck, tail root, back, and full tail are present and readable: pass.
8. There are no wings, collar, cross, religious symbols, text, watermark, or copied character traits: pass.

The scale panel confirms that the cat can sit outside the girl's shoulder line without covering her face.

## Layer and edge check

The eight layers were separated only from the approved complete master. No independent body parts were regenerated. Neck, ear-root, tail-root, back, limb, and eye-swap overlap areas were repaired from the same source. The closed-eye overlay and soft glow are project-authored derived additions.

The master was inspected against black, white, and deep-purple backgrounds. The 1× and 2× extreme boards cover whole-body float ±2.5%, head ±3°, both ears ±6°, and tail ±8°. All extremes remain continuous with no visible gaps, doubled outlines, green fringe, ordering errors, or unacceptable deformation.
